April 27, 2010
 

Blue Ribbon Event Roundup

About 250 guests gathered at the Lehmann Mansion in Lake Villa, Ill., on April 15, for One Hope United's 9th Annual Blue Ribbon Event fundraiser.

The luncheon is the agency's largest fundraiser of the year and raised over $85,000 for its child abuse prevention and therapeutic programs.

"We really rely on private funds to get these services to kids," said One Hope United Executive Director Mark McHugh.

One Hope United's Northern Region serves about 27,000 children and families annually among its programs.

"The event also raises public awareness of child abuse and neglect, which is affecting a growing number of children and families throughout the region's coverage area of Lake County and Wisconsin," McHugh added.

Honorary Chair Cecilia Rodhe spoke to Blue Ribbon guests about her mission to assist at-risk youth. She, along with her son, Chicago Bulls center Joakim Noah, founded Noah's Arc Foundation to support these children through the arts and sports. Noah sent a special message to the OHU kids and guests. To see Noah's video message, click here.  

Kathryn Comstock, a former resident of the agency, also shared her story of courage after abuse and neglect prior to coming to live in the residential program in Lake Villa. She urged everyone to get involved to positively impact the life of a child.  

Comstock spent her high school years at One Hope United and said it was "the safest, happiest time" of her life up to that point. She graduated from college magna cum laude and is now the executive director of Kenosha, Wis.-based Women and Children's Horizons, which supports and educates victims of sexual and domestic violence and abuse.  

"Through intervention from an agency like One Hope United [kids] can learn that life doesn't have to be violent, abusive or hand to mouth," she said. "They can learn that they can design their own lives and go on to be productive members of society."  

Guests enjoyed music by the Lakes Area swing band, raffles, a silent auction and an energetic live auction during which Bulls tickets raised $7,400.  

One Hope United would like to thank its wonderful sponsors, including BMO/Harris, Bauer's Catering, CNA, GE Capital, Inspiring Images Photography, Noah's Arc Foundation, and Sidley Austin LLP, along with many others.
